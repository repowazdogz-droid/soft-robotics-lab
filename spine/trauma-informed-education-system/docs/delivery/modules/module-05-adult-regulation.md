# Module 5: Supporting Adult Regulation

## Purpose

This module helps adults understand and support their own regulation so they can support children. It focuses on stress load, capacity, and practical strategies for self-regulation.

## Intended Audience

All staff

## Time Range

40â€“50 minutes (flexible based on available time)

## What Participants Should Leave With

- Understanding of adult regulation and its importance
- Simple strategies for self-regulation
- How to notice stress and overload
- How to ask for support
- How systems can support adult regulation
- Recognition that regulation varies day to day

## Session Shape

### 40-Minute Variant

**Reading and orientation (25 minutes):**
- Read pillar on adult regulation capacity
- Review operating assumptions about adults
- Understand stress load vs skill deficit
- Review practical strategies

**Reflection and application (15 minutes):**
- Optional reflection on your own regulation
- Consider one strategy
- No personal disclosure required

### 50-Minute Variant

**Reading and orientation (30 minutes):**
- Read pillar on adult regulation capacity
- Review operating assumptions about adults
- Understand stress load vs skill deficit
- Review practical strategies
- Review system support for regulation

**Reflection and application (20 minutes):**
- Optional reflection on your own regulation
- Consider strategies and system support
- Plan one small change
- No personal disclosure required

## Optional Activities

### Activity 1: Regulation Self-Assessment (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Notice your own regulation patterns

**Task:** Reflect on your regulation patterns (optional, no sharing required).

**Questions to consider:**
- What helps you regulate?
- What makes regulation harder?
- How do you notice when you're stressed?
- What support do you need?

**Guidelines:**
- Personal reflection only
- No pressure to share
- Focus on practical strategies
- No personal disclosure required

---

### Activity 2: Strategy Planning (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Plan practical regulation strategies

**Task:** Choose one regulation strategy and plan how to use it.

**Consider:**
- When might you use this strategy?
- How will you remember to use it?
- What support do you need?
- How can systems support you?

**Guidelines:**
- Focus on practical strategies
- Consider system support
- No personal disclosure required

---

### Activity 3: Support Request Planning (5 minutes)

**Purpose:** Plan how to ask for support

**Task:** Consider how you might ask for support when needed.

**Questions to consider:**
- Who can you ask for support?
- How do you ask for support?
- What support do you need?
- How do systems support you?

**Guidelines:**
- Focus on practical support
- Consider professional boundaries
- No personal disclosure required

## Common Misuse Patterns + Redirection

### Misuse: Individual Responsibility Only

**Pattern:** Expecting adults to regulate without systemic support.

**Redirection:** Systems must support adult regulation. Leaders create conditions. Individuals use strategies within supportive systems.

---

### Misuse: Skill Focus Only

**Pattern:** Teaching regulation techniques without reducing stress load.

**Redirection:** Reduce stress load AND teach techniques. Support capacity, not just skills. Create systems that reduce load.

---

### Misuse: Perfectionism

**Pattern:** Expecting adults to be perfectly regulated all the time.

**Redirection:** Regulation varies day to day. Stress affects regulation. Support regulation, don't expect perfection.

---

### Misuse: Blame

**Pattern:** Blaming adults for dysregulation instead of supporting regulation.

**Redirection:** Support regulation. Recognise that stress affects regulation. Create systems that support capacity.

## What This Module Does NOT Do

- Does NOT provide individual therapy
- Does NOT require personal disclosure or sharing
- Does NOT provide clinical assessment
- Does NOT teach therapeutic techniques
- Does NOT promise specific outcomes
- Does NOT replace professional support

## Safeguarding & Boundary Note

This module supports adult regulation. It does NOT replace safeguarding training or procedures. If you have safeguarding concerns, follow your school's safeguarding procedures immediately. Adult regulation supports safeguarding by helping staff respond appropriately, but it does NOT replace safeguarding protocols or professional responsibilities. Always follow safeguarding procedures first.
