# Module 8: Protecting Staff Capacity

## Purpose

This module helps leaders and staff understand and protect staff capacity. It focuses on stress load, capacity limits, and practical strategies for reducing load and increasing support.

## Intended Audience

Leadership and all staff

## Time Range

40â€“50 minutes (flexible based on available time)

## What Participants Should Leave With

- Understanding of capacity and stress load
- How to notice when capacity is exceeded
- How to reduce stress load
- How to protect capacity
- How to ask for support
- Recognition that capacity is finite and varies

## Session Shape

### 40-Minute Variant

**Reading and orientation (25 minutes):**
- Read operating assumptions about adults
- Understand capacity and stress load
- Review strategies for reducing load
- Understand system support for capacity

**Reflection and application (15 minutes):**
- Optional reflection on capacity
- Consider one strategy
- No personal disclosure required

### 50-Minute Variant

**Reading and orientation (30 minutes):**
- Read operating assumptions about adults
- Understand capacity and stress load
- Review strategies for reducing load
- Understand system support for capacity
- Review time protection rules

**Reflection and application (20 minutes):**
- Optional reflection on capacity
- Consider strategies and system support
- Plan one small change
- No personal disclosure required

## Optional Activities

### Activity 1: Capacity Self-Assessment (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Notice your own capacity patterns

**Task:** Reflect on your capacity patterns (optional, no sharing required).

**Questions to consider:**
- What affects your capacity?
- How do you notice when capacity is exceeded?
- What increases your stress load?
- What reduces your stress load?

**Guidelines:**
- Personal reflection only
- No pressure to share
- Focus on practical strategies
- No personal disclosure required

---

### Activity 2: Load Reduction Planning (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Plan how to reduce stress load

**Task:** Consider what creates stress load and how to reduce it.

**Questions to consider:**
- What creates stress load for you?
- What can be reduced or simplified?
- What support do you need?
- How can systems support you?

**Guidelines:**
- Focus on practical strategies
- Consider system support
- No personal disclosure required

---

### Activity 3: System Support Planning (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Plan how systems can support capacity

**Task:** Consider how systems can reduce stress load and support capacity.

**Questions to consider:**
- How can systems reduce stress load?
- What resources support capacity?
- What processes protect capacity?
- How can leaders support capacity?

**Guidelines:**
- Focus on systems, not individuals
- Consider practical constraints
- No personal disclosure required

## Common Misuse Patterns + Redirection

### Misuse: Individual Responsibility Only

**Pattern:** Expecting individuals to manage capacity without systemic support.

**Redirection:** Systems must support capacity. Leaders create conditions. Individuals manage capacity within supportive systems.

---

### Misuse: Blame for Capacity Limits

**Pattern:** Blaming individuals when capacity is exceeded.

**Redirection:** Capacity limits are normal, not failure. Support capacity. Reduce stress load. Create systems that protect capacity.

---

### Misuse: Perfectionism

**Pattern:** Expecting unlimited capacity or perfect performance.

**Redirection:** Capacity is finite and varies. Support capacity. Recognise limits. Protect capacity where possible.

---

### Misuse: Adding Without Removing

**Pattern:** Adding new demands without reducing existing demands.

**Redirection:** Remove before adding. Protect capacity. Reduce stress load. Don't add without removing.

Always follow safeguarding procedures first.
