# Module 7: Working with Parents and Carers

## Purpose

This module supports staff to work effectively with parents while maintaining boundaries. It focuses on shared language, clear roles, and appropriate communication.

## Intended Audience

All staff who work with parents

## What Participants Should Leave With

- Shared language with parents
- Clear role boundaries
- What schools should and shouldn't ask of parents
- How to communicate effectively
- Confidence to work with parents appropriately

- Optional reflection on parent communication

- No personal disclosure required

## Optional Activities

### Activity 1: Shared Language Practice (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Practice using shared language with parents

**Task:** Read a scenario and practice using shared, non-clinical language.

**Example scenario:** "A parent asks why their child is 'dysregulated' at school."

**Shared language:** "Your child appears to be experiencing stress at school. We're working to create predictable, supportive environments that help all children manage stress and learn."

**Guidelines:**
- Use descriptive, non-clinical language
- Focus on support, not diagnosis
- No personal disclosure required

---

### Activity 2: Boundary Clarification (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Understand role boundaries with parents

**Task:** Review boundary scenarios and consider appropriate responses.

**Questions to consider:**
- What are appropriate boundaries?
- What should schools ask of parents?
- What shouldn't schools ask of parents?
- How do boundaries protect everyone?

**Guidelines:**
- Focus on professional boundaries
- Consider safeguarding implications
- No personal disclosure required

---

### Activity 3: Communication Planning (5 minutes)

**Purpose:** Plan effective communication with parents

**Task:** Consider how to communicate clearly and respectfully with parents.

**Questions to consider:**
- How do I communicate clearly?
- How do I maintain boundaries?
- How do I work in partnership?
- What information should I share?

**Guidelines:**
- Focus on clear, respectful communication
- Consider confidentiality and safeguarding
- No personal disclosure required

## Common Misuse Patterns + Redirection

### Misuse: Asking Parents for Therapy

**Pattern:** Asking parents to provide therapeutic interventions or clinical support.

**Redirection:** Schools support children in school contexts. Parents support children in home contexts. Neither replaces the other. Refer to appropriate professionals for therapy.

---

### Misuse: Blaming Parents

**Pattern:** Blaming parents for children's challenges or behaviour.

**Redirection:** Work in partnership. Recognise that both home and school matter. Focus on support, not blame.

---

### Misuse: Excessive Responsibility

**Pattern:** Asking parents to take responsibility for things that are the school's responsibility.

**Redirection:** Clarify roles and responsibilities. Schools are responsible for school contexts. Parents are responsible for home contexts. Work together, don't shift responsibility.

---

### Misuse: Compliance Pressure

**Pattern:** Pressuring parents to comply with approaches that don't work for their family.

**Redirection:** Work in partnership. Adapt approaches where possible. Respect family context and choices.

## What This Module Does NOT Do

- Does NOT provide family therapy guidance
- Does NOT provide clinical interventions
- Does NOT provide parenting advice
- Does NOT require personal disclosure or sharing
- Does NOT promise specific outcomes
- Does NOT replace safeguarding training

## Safeguarding & Boundary Note

This module supports work with parents. It does NOT replace safeguarding training or procedures. If you have safeguarding concerns, follow your school's safeguarding procedures immediately. Working with parents supports safeguarding by maintaining appropriate relationships and communication, but it does NOT replace safeguarding protocols or professional responsibilities. Always follow safeguarding procedures first.
