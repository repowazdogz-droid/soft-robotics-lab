# Release, Exposure & Distribution Control Contract

This contract ensures Omega outputs are exposed deliberately, safely, and appropriately, with clear audience boundaries and no accidental overreach.

Version: 1.0  
Effective: 2024-12-13

---

## 1. Release Principle

Release must adhere to these principles:

### Exposure Is Intentional
- All releases are deliberate and planned
- No accidental or unintended exposure
- Release decisions are explicit and documented
- Intentionality is mandatory

### Scope Precedes Scale
- Scope is defined before scale is considered
- Limited scope is preferred over broad scale
- Scale expansion requires scope validation
- Scope takes precedence over reach

### Trust Beats Reach
- Trusted, limited audiences are preferred
- Reach is not prioritized over trust
- Trust relationships are valued over scale
- Trust is a release requirement

### Reversibility Is Mandatory
- All releases must be reversible
- Revocation mechanisms must exist
- Rollback procedures must be documented
- Irreversible releases are prohibited

---

## 2. What Counts as a Release

A release includes the following:

### Public Artifacts
- Artifacts made publicly available
- Public documentation or content
- Public-facing outputs or interfaces
- Any artifact accessible to public

### Client Deliverables
- Outputs delivered to clients
- Client-facing materials or systems
- Client-accessible content or interfaces
- Any output provided to clients

### Shared Datasets
- Datasets shared with external parties
- Data provided to collaborators or researchers
- Datasets made available to third parties
- Any dataset shared outside internal use

### Demos
- Demonstrations shown to external audiences
- Demo materials or presentations
- Interactive demonstrations
- Any demo accessible to external parties

### Pilots
- Pilot programs with external participants
- Pilot systems or capabilities
- Pilot deployments or trials
- Any pilot involving external parties

### Presentations
- Presentations to external audiences
- Conference talks or public speaking
- External training or workshops
- Any presentation to external parties

### Research Disclosures
- Research findings shared externally
- Academic publications or preprints
- Research presentations or posters
- Any research content shared externally

### Learning Environments
- Learning materials shared externally
- Educational content or courses
- Training materials for external use
- Any learning content shared externally

### Simulations Accessible to Others
- Simulations made available to external users
- Simulation platforms or tools
- Simulation results or outputs
- Any simulation accessible to external parties

### Internal Drafts Are Not Releases

- Internal drafts and work-in-progress are not releases
- Internal-only materials do not require release procedures
- Internal collaboration is not considered release
- Only external-facing outputs are releases

---

## 3. Audience Classification

Every release must declare one audience:

### Private (You Only)
- Release is accessible only to the operator
- No external access or sharing
- Private use only
- No distribution beyond operator

### Internal (Omega System)
- Release is accessible within Omega system
- Internal system components only
- No external access
- System-internal use only

### Trusted Collaborator
- Release is accessible to trusted collaborators
- Collaborators are explicitly identified
- Trust relationship is documented
- Limited, trusted audience

### Client
- Release is accessible to specific clients
- Clients are explicitly identified
- Client relationship is documented
- Client-specific access

### Limited Public
- Release is accessible to limited public audience
- Access restrictions are defined
- Audience boundaries are explicit
- Limited, controlled public access

### Open Public
- Release is accessible to general public
- No access restrictions
- Publicly available
- Open, unrestricted access

### No Default to Public

- Default audience is never public
- Public release requires explicit declaration
- Audience must be explicitly chosen
- No assumption of public access

---

## 4. Exposure Boundaries

For each release, specify:

### Who Can Access
- Explicit list of who can access the release
- Access controls and authentication
- Access restrictions and limitations
- Access boundaries are clearly defined

### What Can Be Reused
- What parts of the release can be reused
- Reuse permissions and licenses
- Reuse restrictions and conditions
- Reuse boundaries are clearly defined

### What Cannot Be Inferred
- What conclusions cannot be drawn from the release
- What inferences are not supported
- What claims cannot be made
- Inference boundaries are clearly defined

### What Is Out of Scope
- What is explicitly not included in the release
- What capabilities are not demonstrated
- What claims are not made
- Scope boundaries are clearly defined

### Ambiguity Blocks Release

- Ambiguous boundaries block release
- All boundaries must be explicit
- Unclear boundaries are not acceptable
- Release requires clear boundary definition

---

## 5. Risk Assessment

Before release, assess:

### Misuse Potential
- How could the release be misused?
- What malicious use cases exist?
- What abuse vectors are possible?
- Misuse potential must be assessed

### Misinterpretation Risk
- How could the release be misunderstood?
- What misinterpretations are likely?
- What confusion could result?
- Misinterpretation risk must be assessed

### Reputational Risk
- What reputational risks exist?
- How could the release affect reputation?
- What negative associations are possible?
- Reputational risk must be assessed

### Safety Impact
- What safety risks does the release create?
- How could the release cause harm?
- What safety considerations apply?
- Safety impact must be assessed

### Regulatory Sensitivity
- What regulatory considerations apply?
- What compliance risks exist?
- What legal or regulatory issues are possible?
- Regulatory sensitivity must be assessed

### High Risk Requires Restriction or Redesign

- High-risk releases must be restricted or redesigned
- High risk cannot be ignored
- Risk mitigation is mandatory
- High risk blocks unrestricted release

---

## 6. Tiered Disclosure

Prefer:

### Minimal Viable Exposure
- Expose only what is necessary
- Minimal exposure is preferred
- Avoid over-exposure
- Minimal viable exposure is the default

### Partial Datasets
- Share partial datasets when possible
- Avoid full dataset exposure
- Partial data reduces risk
- Partial datasets are preferred

### Constrained Demos
- Demos with constraints and limitations
- Avoid full capability demonstrations
- Constrained demos reduce overreach
- Constrained demos are preferred

### Time-Limited Access
- Access that expires after time period
- Avoid permanent access when possible
- Time limits reduce long-term risk
- Time-limited access is preferred

### Capability Ceilings
- Explicit limits on demonstrated capabilities
- Avoid showing full system capability
- Capability ceilings prevent overreach
- Capability ceilings are preferred

### Never Expose Full Capability Unnecessarily

- Full capability exposure is rarely necessary
- Avoid exposing full capabilities
- Partial capability exposure is preferred
- Full capability exposure requires strong justification

---

## 7. Attribution & Framing

All releases must:

### State Purpose
- Explicitly state the purpose of the release
- Purpose is clear and honest
- No hidden purposes or agendas
- Purpose statement is mandatory

### State Limits
- Explicitly state limitations of the release
- Limits are clear and comprehensive
- No hidden limitations
- Limit statement is mandatory

### Avoid Hype
- No exaggerated claims or promises
- No marketing language or superlatives
- No overstatement of capabilities
- Hype is prohibited

### Avoid Autonomy Claims
- No claims of autonomous operation
- No suggestions of independent decision-making
- No implications of self-directed behavior
- Autonomy claims are prohibited

### Avoid Predictive Guarantees
- No guarantees about future outcomes
- No promises of specific results
- No predictive certainty claims
- Predictive guarantees are prohibited

### Marketing Language Is Forbidden

- Marketing language is not permitted
- Promotional content is prohibited
- Sales-oriented framing is forbidden
- Releases must be factual and honest

---

## 8. Revocation & Rollback

Every release must have:

### Revocation Mechanism
- Clear procedure for revoking release
- Revocation can be executed
- Revocation is documented
- Revocation mechanism is mandatory

### Access Removal Plan
- Plan for removing access to release
- Access removal procedures are documented
- Access removal can be executed
- Access removal plan is mandatory

### Version Control
- Release versions are tracked
- Version history is maintained
- Version changes are documented
- Version control is mandatory

### Deprecation Notice
- Procedure for deprecating release
- Deprecation notices are provided
- Deprecation timeline is communicated
- Deprecation notice is mandatory

### If Rollback Is Impossible â†’ Do Not Release

- Releases without rollback are prohibited
- Rollback must be possible
- Impossible rollback blocks release
- Rollback capability is mandatory

---

## 9. Monitoring & Feedback

After release:

### Monitor Usage
- Monitor how release is used
- Track access patterns and usage
- Identify unusual or concerning usage
- Usage monitoring is mandatory

### Collect Feedback
- Collect feedback from users
- Gather user experiences and concerns
- Document feedback and responses
- Feedback collection is mandatory

### Watch for Misuse
- Monitor for misuse or abuse
- Identify unauthorized or harmful use
- Detect violation of boundaries
- Misuse monitoring is mandatory

### Update Risk Assessment
- Update risk assessment based on monitoring
- Revise risk assessment as needed
- Adjust restrictions if necessary
- Risk assessment updates are mandatory

### Silence Does Not Imply Safety

- Absence of feedback does not mean safety
- No feedback is not evidence of safety
- Monitoring must be active
- Silence requires investigation, not assumption

---

## 10. Change Control

This contract may only change via:

### Explicit Revision
- Contract changes must be explicit and documented
- No ad-hoc edits to release principles or requirements
- Changes require version control
- Changes are traceable and auditable

### Documented Rationale
- Every contract change must include rationale:
  - What changed in the contract
  - Why it changed
  - What it affects
  - Expected impact on release and distribution

### Rollback Path
- Every contract change must have rollback path
- Previous contract versions must be preserved
- Rollback procedure must be documented
- Rollback can be executed at any time

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this contract include:
- Releasing without audience classification
- Releasing with ambiguous boundaries
- Releasing without risk assessment
- Using marketing language in releases
- Releasing without revocation mechanism
- Missing or incomplete monitoring
- Releasing without rollback capability

Violations must be:
- Logged as incidents
- Reviewed by system owner
- Corrected immediately
- Documented in change logs

---

## Amendments

Changes to release principles, audience classification, exposure boundaries, or risk assessment require:
- Impact assessment on release safety and appropriateness
- Testing with representative releases
- Approval from system owner
- Version increment
- Documentation in change logs
