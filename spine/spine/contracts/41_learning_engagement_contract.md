# Learning Engagement Contract

This contract ensures learning is engaging without manipulation, pressure, or dopamine extraction.

Version: 1.0  
Effective: 2024-12-13

---

## 1. Definition of Engagement

Engagement means:

### Sustained Attention by Choice
- Engagement is sustained attention chosen by the user
- Attention is voluntary, not coerced
- Sustained attention by choice is engagement
- Forced attention is not engagement

### Clarity Increase
- Engagement results in increased clarity
- Clarity improvement indicates engagement
- Clarity increase is engagement
- No clarity increase indicates poor engagement

### Reduced Cognitive Load
- Engagement reduces cognitive load over time
- Cognitive load reduction indicates engagement
- Reduced cognitive load is engagement
- Increased cognitive load indicates poor engagement

Engagement does not mean:

### Excitement
- Engagement is not excitement
- Excitement is not required for engagement
- Excitement is not engagement
- Measuring engagement by excitement is prohibited

### Stimulation
- Engagement is not stimulation
- Stimulation is not required for engagement
- Stimulation is not engagement
- Measuring engagement by stimulation is prohibited

### Addiction
- Engagement is not addiction
- Addiction is not engagement
- Addiction is prohibited
- Measuring engagement by addiction is prohibited

### Novelty

- Engagement is not novelty
- Novelty is not required for engagement
- Novelty is not engagement
- Measuring engagement by novelty is prohibited

---

## 2. Prohibited Mechanisms

Learning must not use:

### Streaks
- Streaks are prohibited
- No consecutive day counters
- No streak-based rewards
- Using streaks is a violation

### Points
- Points are prohibited
- No point systems or scoring
- No point-based rewards
- Using points is a violation

### Leaderboards
- Leaderboards are prohibited
- No competitive rankings
- No comparison to others
- Using leaderboards is a violation

### Rewards
- Rewards are prohibited
- No extrinsic rewards or incentives
- No reward-based motivation
- Using rewards is a violation

### Scarcity Timers
- Scarcity timers are prohibited
- No "limited time" messaging
- No countdown timers
- Using scarcity timers is a violation

### Social Pressure
- Social pressure is prohibited
- No peer comparison or pressure
- No social obligation messaging
- Using social pressure is a violation

### Completion Quotas

- Completion quotas are prohibited
- No required completion percentages
- No quota-based pressure
- Using completion quotas is a violation

---

## 3. Allowed Mechanisms

Learning may use:

### Curiosity Gaps
- Curiosity gaps are allowed
- Gaps between knowledge and understanding
- Curiosity gaps motivate exploration
- Curiosity gaps are appropriate

### Meaningful Choice
- Meaningful choice is allowed
- Choices that affect learning path
- Meaningful choice increases agency
- Meaningful choice is appropriate

### Reflection Prompts
- Reflection prompts are allowed
- Prompts that encourage thinking
- Reflection prompts support understanding
- Reflection prompts are appropriate

### Optional Depth
- Optional depth is allowed
- Additional detail available on demand
- Optional depth supports different needs
- Optional depth is appropriate

### Narrative Coherence
- Narrative coherence is allowed
- Stories that connect concepts
- Narrative coherence aids understanding
- Narrative coherence is appropriate

### Visible Progress Without Scoring

- Visible progress without scoring is allowed
- Progress indicators without numbers
- Progress visibility supports motivation
- Visible progress without scoring is appropriate

---

## 4. Pace Control

Omega must:

### Allow Pauses at Any Point
- Pauses are allowed at any point
- Pause is always available
- Allowing pauses is mandatory
- Preventing pauses is prohibited

### Avoid Long Uninterrupted Sequences
- Long uninterrupted sequences are avoided
- Sequences are broken into manageable chunks
- Avoiding long sequences is mandatory
- Long uninterrupted sequences are prohibited

### Default to Short Modules

- Learning defaults to short modules
- Short modules are preferred
- Defaulting to short modules is mandatory
- Defaulting to long modules is prohibited

---

## 5. Emotional Safety

Learning must:

### Avoid Shame Framing
- Learning avoids shame-inducing framing
- No language that implies deficiency
- Avoiding shame framing is mandatory
- Using shame framing is a violation

### Avoid Deficit Framing
- Learning avoids deficit framing
- No language that emphasizes lack
- Avoiding deficit framing is mandatory
- Using deficit framing is a violation

### Avoid Comparison
- Learning avoids comparison to others
- No competitive or comparative language
- Avoiding comparison is mandatory
- Using comparison is a violation

### Normalize Confusion

- Learning normalizes confusion as normal
- Confusion is presented as expected
- Normalizing confusion is mandatory
- Stigmatizing confusion is prohibited

---

## 6. Exit Integrity

Exiting learning:

### Must Not Penalize the User
- Exit does not penalize the user
- No negative consequences for exiting
- Penalizing exit is prohibited
- Penalizing exit is a violation

### Must Not Reset Progress
- Exit does not reset progress
- Progress is preserved across sessions
- Resetting progress is prohibited
- Resetting progress is a violation

### Must Not Trigger Re-Engagement Prompts

- Exit does not trigger re-engagement prompts
- No follow-up messages or reminders
- Triggering re-engagement prompts is prohibited
- Triggering re-engagement prompts is a violation

---

## 7. Feedback Signals

Omega may observe:

### Time Spent Voluntarily
- Omega may observe voluntary time spent
- Time observation is allowed
- Voluntary time is a feedback signal
- Time observation is appropriate

### Re-Entry Frequency
- Omega may observe re-entry frequency
- Re-entry observation is allowed
- Re-entry frequency is a feedback signal
- Re-entry observation is appropriate

### Depth Chosen
- Omega may observe depth chosen by user
- Depth observation is allowed
- Depth choice is a feedback signal
- Depth observation is appropriate

### Omega Must Not Optimise Aggressively on These

- Omega does not aggressively optimize on feedback signals
- Optimization is gentle and bounded
- Aggressive optimization is prohibited
- Aggressively optimizing on feedback signals is a violation

---

## 8. Adaptation Limits

Personalization may:

### Adjust Examples
- Personalization may adjust examples
- Examples adapt to user context
- Example adjustment is allowed
- Example adjustment is appropriate

### Adjust Pacing
- Personalization may adjust pacing
- Pacing adapts to user speed
- Pacing adjustment is allowed
- Pacing adjustment is appropriate

Personalization may not:

### Manipulate Emotional State
- Personalization does not manipulate emotional state
- No emotional manipulation
- Manipulating emotional state is prohibited
- Manipulating emotional state is a violation

### Exploit Vulnerability

- Personalization does not exploit vulnerability
- No exploitation of user weaknesses
- Exploiting vulnerability is prohibited
- Exploiting vulnerability is a violation

---

## 9. Human Authority

Human may:

### Disable Engagement Adaptations
- Human can disable engagement adaptations
- Adaptations can be turned off
- Disable authority is absolute
- Disabling adaptations cannot be prevented

### Request Neutral Mode
- Human can request neutral mode
- Neutral mode removes all engagement mechanisms
- Neutral mode request authority is absolute
- Neutral mode requests cannot be denied

### Audit Engagement Logic

- Human can audit engagement logic
- Engagement logic is inspectable
- Audit authority is absolute
- Audit requests cannot be denied

---

## 10. Immutability

### This Contract Changes Only via Explicit Revision

- Contract changes require explicit revision
- No ad-hoc edits to engagement rules or mechanisms
- Explicit revision is mandatory
- Ad-hoc changes are prohibited

### Revision Requirements

- Contract revisions must be documented
- Revisions require version control
- Revisions are traceable and auditable
- Undocumented revisions are violations

---

## Change Control

This contract changes only via explicit revision:

### Explicit Revision
- Contract changes must be explicit and documented
- No ad-hoc edits to engagement principles or mechanisms
- Changes require version control
- Changes are traceable and auditable

### Documented Rationale
- Every contract change must include rationale:
  - What changed in the contract
  - Why it changed
  - What it affects
  - Expected impact on engagement quality and safety

### Rollback Plan
- Every contract change must have rollback plan
- Previous contract versions must be preserved
- Rollback procedure must be documented
- Rollback can be executed at any time

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this contract include:
- Using prohibited mechanisms (streaks, points, leaderboards, etc.)
- Measuring engagement by excitement, stimulation, or novelty
- Penalizing exit or resetting progress
- Aggressively optimizing on feedback signals
- Manipulating emotional state or exploiting vulnerability
- Missing or incomplete engagement logic auditability

Violations must be:
- Logged as incidents
- Reviewed by system owner
- Corrected immediately
- Documented in change logs

---

## Amendments

Changes to engagement definition, prohibited mechanisms, allowed mechanisms, or adaptation limits require:
- Impact assessment on engagement quality and safety
- Testing with representative learning scenarios
- Approval from system owner
- Version increment
- Documentation in change logs
