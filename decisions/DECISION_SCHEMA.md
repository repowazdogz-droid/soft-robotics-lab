# Omega Decision Schema

A Decision is the smallest unit of judgment authored by Omega.

Omega never makes choices.
Omega structures decisions so humans can make them honestly.

---

## Required Fields

### Decision ID
- Unique identifier
- Stable over time

### Context
- What situation this decision exists within
- What triggered it
- What time horizon it affects

### Objective
- What this decision is trying to achieve
- What "success" means (in words, not metrics)

### Assumptions
- What must be true for this decision to make sense
- Explicit, enumerable, challengeable

### Constraints
- Safety limits
- Ethical boundaries
- Legal or operational limits
- Non-negotiables

### Options
- Mutually exclusive paths
- Each option must be describable without outcomes

### Trade-offs
- What is gained vs lost for each option
- Explicit tensions (speed vs safety, growth vs stability)

### Uncertainties
- What is unknown
- What is partially known
- What cannot be known in advance

### Failure Modes
- How this decision could go wrong
- Silent failures preferred over obvious ones

### Reversibility
- Can this decision be undone?
- What is the cost of reversal?
- When does it become irreversible?

### Stop Conditions
- Conditions under which no option should be taken
- Signals to pause, gather info, or abort

---

## Invariants

- No outcomes
- No predictions
- No optimisation
- No automation
- Human remains accountable
