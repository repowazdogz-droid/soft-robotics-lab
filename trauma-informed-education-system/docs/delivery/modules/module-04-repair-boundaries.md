# Module 4: Repair After Rupture

## Purpose

This module supports staff to repair relationships and maintain boundaries when things go wrong. It focuses on acknowledgment, responsibility, and restoration without therapeutic techniques.

## Intended Audience

All staff

## Time Range

30â€“40 minutes (flexible based on available time)

## What Participants Should Leave With

- Simple language for repair
- Process for acknowledging mistakes and restoring relationships
- How to maintain professional boundaries
- When and how to escalate
- Confidence to repair ruptures appropriately

## Session Shape

### 30-Minute Variant

**Reading and orientation (20 minutes):**
- Read pillar on repair and learning loops
- Review process map for repair
- Understand repair language and timing

**Reflection and application (10 minutes):**
- Optional reflection on repair in practice
- Consider one repair scenario
- No personal disclosure required

### 40-Minute Variant

**Reading and orientation (25 minutes):**
- Read pillar on repair and learning loops
- Review process map for repair
- Understand repair language and timing
- Review boundary maintenance

**Reflection and application (15 minutes):**
- Optional reflection on repair in practice
- Consider repair scenarios
- Practice repair language
- No personal disclosure required

## Optional Activities

### Activity 1: Repair Language Practice (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Practice using repair language

**Task:** Read a scenario and practice repair language.

**Example scenario:** "A child felt ignored when you didn't respond to their question immediately."

**Repair language:** "I notice that I didn't respond to your question right away. I'm sorry that happened. Help me understand what that was like for you."

**Guidelines:**
- Focus on acknowledgment and responsibility
- Use simple, clear language
- No personal disclosure required

---

### Activity 2: Boundary Reflection (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Understand professional boundaries

**Task:** Review boundary scenarios and consider appropriate responses.

**Questions to consider:**
- What are appropriate boundaries?
- When should boundaries be maintained?
- When should boundaries be adjusted?
- How do boundaries protect everyone?

**Guidelines:**
- Focus on professional boundaries
- Consider safeguarding implications
- No personal disclosure required

---

### Activity 3: Escalation Planning (5 minutes)

**Purpose:** Understand when and how to escalate

**Task:** Review escalation scenarios and consider appropriate responses.

**Questions to consider:**
- When should I escalate?
- How do I escalate appropriately?
- What information should I share?
- Who should I escalate to?

**Guidelines:**
- Focus on safeguarding and professional boundaries
- Consider school procedures
- No personal disclosure required

## Common Misuse Patterns + Redirection

### Misuse: Avoiding Repair

**Pattern:** Avoiding repair because it's uncomfortable or difficult.

**Redirection:** Repair is necessary for relationship restoration. Small repairs prevent larger ruptures. Repair when it's safe and appropriate.

---

### Misuse: Blame Focus

**Pattern:** Focusing on blame instead of repair.

**Redirection:** Focus on acknowledgment, responsibility, and restoration. Blame prevents repair. Take responsibility for your part.

---

### Misuse: Therapeutic Repair

**Pattern:** Using therapeutic techniques or requiring emotional disclosure for repair.

**Redirection:** Use simple, clear language. Acknowledge, take responsibility, and restore. No therapeutic techniques needed.

---

### Misuse: Boundary Blurring

**Pattern:** Blurring professional boundaries in the name of repair.

**Redirection:** Maintain professional boundaries. Repair relationships within professional boundaries. Boundaries protect everyone.

## What This Module Does NOT Do

- Does NOT provide therapeutic repair techniques
- Does NOT require personal disclosure or sharing
- Does NOT provide personal relationship guidance
- Does NOT teach clinical interventions
- Does NOT replace safeguarding training
- Does NOT promise specific outcomes

## Safeguarding & Boundary Note

This module supports relationship repair and boundary maintenance. It does NOT replace safeguarding training or procedures. If you have safeguarding concerns, follow your school's safeguarding procedures immediately. Repair supports safeguarding by maintaining appropriate relationships and boundaries, but it does NOT replace safeguarding protocols or professional responsibilities. Always follow safeguarding procedures first.
