## Core Innovation Statement

**From OPS/FULL_INNOVATION_BIOS.md:**

"Protocols that make decision structure visible—assumptions, ownership gaps, and stop conditions—before action hardens into outcomes.

Omega Protocols address a specific gap: high-stakes decisions are made under pressure, but the structure of those decisions (who owns what, what must be assumed, what would trigger a pause) remains invisible. ## The Protocols

### Omega (Base Protocol)
- Decision, owner, stop conditions
- Turns messy situations into decisions someone can own
- Produces: Decision brief, responsibility map, stop conditions, open questions

### Omega-A (Alignment)
**What it inspects:**
- Decision-boundary alignment: mandate, authority signals, decision substitution, value injection, and control preservation. It identifies where decisions are made, who has authority, and how boundaries are maintained.

**When to use:**
- Use Omega-A when you need to identify hidden assumptions or dependencies. It's particularly useful for:
  - Uncovering implicit premises in arguments or claims
  - Identifying dependencies in system designs
  - Examining prerequisites for decisions or strategies

**Structure:**
- Explicit assumptions: Premises that are stated but may be unexamined
- Implicit assumptions: Premises that are unstated but necessary
- Dependencies: Conditions that must hold for the assumption to be valid
- Uncertainty: Where assumptions are uncertain or unverifiable

**Where it applies:**
- Omega-A applies wherever assumptions need to be surfaced: policy analysis, system design, strategic planning, and research evaluation. It helps identify what must be true for claims or decisions to hold.

### Omega-E (Epistemics)
**What it inspects:**
- Epistemic state extraction: what is known, unknown, uncertain, and why. It structures knowledge states and identifies the basis for epistemic claims.

**When to use:**
- Use Omega-E when you need to distinguish what's shown from what's implied, or to identify evidence boundaries. It's particularly useful for:
  - Evaluating research evidence or data
  - Distinguishing observation from inference
  - Identifying what can be directly verified

**Structure:**
- Directly shown: What can be observed, measured, or sourced directly
- Implied: What is inferred or concluded from what's shown
- Boundaries: Limits of what the evidence supports
- Gaps: What evidence is missing or unavailable

**Where it applies:**
- Omega-E applies wherever evidence needs structured inspection: research evaluation, data analysis, policy assessment, and technical review. It helps distinguish what's shown from what's inferred.

### Omega-S (Stress)
**What it inspects:**
- Stress testing, scenario completeness, reverse stress, and BAU integration. It examines how systems, claims, or decisions perform under stress and identifies scenario gaps.

**When to use:**
- Use Omega-S when you need to examine system structure, architecture, or design. It's particularly useful for:
  - Analyzing technical architectures or system designs
  - Examining organizational structures or processes
  - Reviewing policy systems or governance structures

**Structure:**
- Components: Elements that make up the system
- Relationships: How components interact or depend on each other
- Constraints: Limits or requirements that bound the system
- Tradeoffs: Choices between competing objectives or constraints

**Where it applies:**
- Omega-S applies wherever systems need structured inspection: technical architecture, organizational design, policy systems, and process analysis. It helps identify components, relationships, constraints, and tradeoffs.

### Omega-U (Uncertainty)
**What it inspects:**
- Uncertainty visibility: bounds, sensitivity, fragility, and confidence qualifiers. It structures uncertainty and identifies where confidence is warranted vs unwarranted.

**When to use:**
- Use Omega-U when you need to identify and structure uncertainty. It's particularly useful for:
  - Identifying unknowns in claims or decisions
  - Structuring ambiguity or unclear information
  - Examining what cannot be determined

**Structure:**
- Known unknowns: What is recognized as uncertain or unknown
- Unknown unknowns: What is not recognized as uncertain
- Ambiguity: Where information is unclear or can be interpreted multiple ways
- Unknowable: What cannot be determined from available information

**Where it applies:**
- Omega-U applies wherever uncertainty needs structured inspection: strategic planning, risk assessment, research evaluation, and decision support. It helps identify and organize what's unknown or unclear.

### Omega-C (Control)
**What it inspects:**
- Control and authority boundaries: override, escalation, autonomy ceilings, and operator agency. It identifies where control resides and how boundaries are enforced.

**When to use:**
- Use Omega-C when you need to identify constraints or boundaries. It's particularly useful for:
  - Identifying limits in claims or proposals
  - Examining boundaries in system designs
  - Understanding requirements or conditions

**Structure:**
- Hard constraints: Limits that cannot be violated
- Soft constraints: Limits that can be relaxed or negotiated
- Boundaries: Edges or limits of scope or applicability
- Requirements: Conditions that must be met

**Where it applies:**
- Omega-C applies wherever constraints need structured inspection: system design, policy analysis, technical architecture, and strategic planning. It helps identify limits, boundaries, and requirements.

## Innovation Characteristics

**1. Inspection Over Prescription**
- Inspects decisions, doesn't prescribe them
- Makes structure visible, doesn't resolve content
- Prevents silent delegation, doesn't automate judgement

**2. Structure Over Strategy**
- Examines decision structure, not strategic direction
- Makes assumptions visible, not just outcomes
- Focuses on "what must be decided" not "what should we do"

**3. Clarity Over Certainty**
- Doesn't manufacture confidence
- Makes uncertainty explicit
- Surfaces what's unknown

**4. Ownership Over Optimization**
- Focuses on who owns decisions, not what's optimal
- Makes responsibility visible
- Creates defensible narratives

## Stewardship

**Core statement:**
- "Omega Protocol is maintained as infrastructure for reasoning under uncertainty. The protocols are designed to be descriptive, not prescriptive — exposing structure without prescribing outcomes."

**Principles:**
- **Neutrality** — Protocols do not favor outcomes or perspectives
- **Clarity** — Structure without unnecessary complexity
- **Accessibility** — Usable without specialized training
- **Stability** — Core structures remain consistent over time

**Governance:**
- Changes to core protocols follow structured review and versioning. Core structures remain stable; examples and applications evolve through use.

**Use, Access, and Pricing:**

**Core statement:**
- "Omega Protocol is open infrastructure."
- "The protocols themselves are free to use, adapt, and apply in any context. No license, subscription, or permission is required."

**How Omega Is Funded:**
- Omega is sustained through **contextual application**, not gated access.
- We engage selectively in:
  - institutional applications
  - industry-specific implementations
  - facilitated workshops or reviews
  - bespoke protocol extensions
  - long-horizon research or policy collaborations

**Pricing Philosophy:**
- There is no universal price list.
- Pricing reflects:
  - scale and complexity of the context
  - institutional capacity and resources
  - duration and depth of engagement
  - whether work is exploratory, applied, or embedded
- Industries and institutions with greater resources subsidize broader access.

**Public & Community Use:**
- Individuals, educators, community groups, and under-resourced contexts are encouraged to use Omega freely.
- No approval is required.
- No reporting is expected.
- No usage is monitored.
- When support is needed, we explore formats that preserve accessibility without introducing dependency or obligation.

**How Engagement Happens:**
- Omega is not sold in a traditional sense.
- Most collaborations begin when:
  - people encounter the protocols
  - recognize their value
  - reach out to explore fit
- If there is alignment, we proceed.
- If not, the protocols remain available regardless.
- Omega is infrastructure first.
- Engagement is optional.
